BACKGROUND

I began my education in visual art and design at the South Carolina Governor's School. As an undergraduate, I studied Art History while working in public programming and producing promotional material at the Kelly Writers House. After a few years flexing my customer service skills and exploring my own artistic practice, I returned to school for an MS in Information Studies. While studying data analysis and program management, I applied my coursework and design background to program evaluation consulting projects. I refined my instruction, research, and reference abilities as a graduate research associate at the Harry Ransom Center.

When presenting information, I am energized by opportunities to collaborate on creative solutions and communicate information in ways that facilitate understanding. I am passionate about improvement through analysis and wholeheartedly believe that powerful, communicative design can drive meaningful action. I aim to put this into practice in my own work while supporting the analysis and presentation activities of others.
